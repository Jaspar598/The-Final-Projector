Creation

This chapter will focus on 2 of the 12 principles of animation, Straight ahead and Pose to Pose and Solid
Drawing.

These principles are more behind the scenes than actual animation. They are simple rules of thumb to help animators accomplish their goals in an efficient and timely manner.

Straight ahead and Pose to Pose

These are two different approaches to creating animation. Straight ahead animation involves drawing each frame in sequence from start to finish, while pose to pose animation involves creating key poses first and then filling in the in-between frames later. Both techniques have their advantages and disadvantages, and animators often use a combination of both to achieve the desired effect.

Solid Drawing

This principle emphasizes the importance of creating characters and objects that have a sense of weight, volume, and three-dimensionality. Animators should strive to create drawings that are well-constructed and believable, with a clear understanding of anatomy, perspective, and lighting. This helps to create a more immersive and engaging experience for the viewer.
