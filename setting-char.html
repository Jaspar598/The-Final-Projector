Settings and Characters

This chapter will focus on two of the 12 principles of animation, Staging and Appeal.

These principles focus on the design and presentation of characters and settings in animation. They are essential for creating engaging and memorable animations that captivate audiences.

Staging

Staging is the principle that focuses on the presentation of characters and settings in a way that is clear and visually appealing. It involves the use of composition, lighting, and camera angles to direct the audience's attention to the most important elements of a scene. For example, a character might be positioned in the center of the frame with dramatic lighting to emphasize their importance. This principle helps to create a strong visual impact and enhance the overall storytelling of the animation.

Appeal

Appeal is the principle that focuses on the design and personality of characters to make them visually interesting and engaging. It involves the use of shapes, colors, and proportions to create characters that are memorable and likable. For example, a character with exaggerated features and a unique color palette can stand out and capture the audience's attention. This principle helps to create a strong emotional connection between the audience and the characters, enhancing the overall impact of the animation.
